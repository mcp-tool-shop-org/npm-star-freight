Star Freight takes place in the Threshold, a politically fractured star system shared by five civilizations. This page covers the world you trade in.

## Five civilizations

| Civilization | Identity | Trade specialty |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Terran Compact** | Bureaucratic human government | Manufactured goods, alloys, medical supplies |
| **Keth Communion** | Arthropod collective, seasonal biology | Bio-crystal, organic compounds, living materials |
| **Veshan Principalities** | Reptilian feudal houses | Weapons, armor, rare minerals |
| **Orryn Drift** | Mobile cephalopod brokers | Information, brokered cross-civilization goods |
| **Sable Reach** | Outlaw power vacuum | Salvage, Ancestor tech, contraband |

### The Terran Compact

The Compact is the human bureaucracy that disgraced you before the game begins. Getting back into their good graces means permits, patience, and swallowing pride. They control manufactured goods and medical supply chains. Their stations are the most regulated and the most predictable.

### The Keth Communion

The Keth are an arthropod collective that operates on a biological calendar. Their seasons change what goods are available, what gift-giving means, and when pushing a deal becomes an insult. The best margins in the system come from understanding Keth timing. The fastest reputation collapse comes from ignoring it.

### The Veshan Principalities

Reptilian feudal houses obsessed with honor and debt. The Veshan Debt Ledger is not flavor text -- it is leverage. Challenges are expected, and refusing one is worse than losing. Veshan space offers weapons, armor, and rare minerals, but every transaction carries social weight.

### The Orryn Drift

A mobile broker civilization of cephalopods. The Orryn trade with everyone, know everything, and charge for both. They do not produce goods -- they move information and broker cross-civilization deals. Working with the Orryn means paying a premium for access you cannot get elsewhere.

### The Sable Reach

Pirate factions, Ancestor tech salvagers, and people the Compact would rather forget. No law, no customs, no refunds. The Sable Reach is where you go when you need something that does not officially exist, or when everywhere else has closed its doors.

## Lane identities

Routes in Star Freight are governed corridors, not open space. Each lane has an identity that determines the risks and rules of travel.

| Identity | Meaning | Risk profile |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Inspected** | Compact-monitored | High contraband detection, low ambush |
| **Convoy** | Protected relief route | Schedule-bound, inspection scrutiny |
| **Contested** | Disputed territory | Ambush risk, faction conflict |
| **Hazard** | Debris, asteroid, nebula | Environmental damage, navigation checks |
| **Gray** | Unpoliced | Highest danger, lowest scrutiny |

Choosing your route is a strategic decision. Inspected lanes are safe but will catch contraband. Gray lanes let you move anything but expose you to pirates. Convoy lanes give you protection at the cost of schedule discipline.

## Captain postures

Your captain evolves into one of three postures based on your decisions. These are not classes you pick at character creation -- they emerge from how you play.

| Posture | Core mechanic | Pressure |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Relief captain** | Convoy access, provision bonuses, trust-based access | Inspection scrutiny, schedule delays, public consequence |
| **Gray runner** | Falsified manifests, timing arbitrage, institutional risk | Seizure exposure, heat accumulation |
| **Honor captain** | Challenge rights, escort premiums, direct confrontation | Standing volatility, escalation risk, house politics |

## The economy

The economy in Star Freight responds to what players and civilizations do. Shortages change prices. Seasons shift supply. Contracts that look simple can become complicated when the paperwork catches up, the shortage changes the price, or the cargo turns out to be evidence.

Crew members are not stat bonuses -- they are the reason you can access certain stations, use certain abilities, and notice certain things. Losing a crew member means losing capability across multiple systems.

## Combat

Combat in Star Freight is a campaign event, not a random encounter. Every fight has consequences:

- **Victory** writes salvage credits and faction heat into your record
- **Retreat** costs jettisoned cargo and a reputation as someone who runs
- **Defeat** means seized cargo, injured crew, and a ship that limps to the nearest station at premium docking fees

There is no "reload and try again." Every outcome persists.
